Linkin Park is an American rock band from Agoura Hills, California. The band's current lineup comprises vocalist/rhythm guitarist/keyboardist Mike Shinoda, lead guitarist Brad Delson, bassist Dave Farrell, DJ/turntablist Joe Hahn and drummer Rob Bourdon, all of whom are founding members. Vocalists Mark Wakefield and Chester Bennington are former members of the band. Categorized as alternative rock, Linkin Park's earlier music spanned a fusion of heavy metal and hip hop, while their later music features more electronica and pop elements.The band became very famous with their first album, Hybrid Theory, which was given a diamond certification by the RIAA for selling more than 10 million copies."Hybrid Theory" became a massive commercial success, selling millions of copies worldwide and producing hit singles like "In the End," "Crawling," and "One Step Closer." The album's success propelled Linkin Park to the forefront of the alternative rock scene in the early 2000s.Over the years, Linkin Park released several more studio albums, including "Meteora" (2003), "Minutes to Midnight" (2007), "A Thousand Suns" (2010), "Living Things" (2012), "The Hunting Party" (2014), and "One More Light" (2017). Each album showcased the band's evolving musical style, experimenting with different sounds and genres while maintaining their signature intensity and introspective lyrics.Tragically, on July 20, 2017, Chester Bennington died by suicide, leaving a profound impact on the band and their fans worldwide. His distinctive voice and emotional performances had become synonymous with Linkin Park's music.

Despite the loss of Bennington, the remaining members of Linkin Park, along with guest vocalists, occasionally perform together in tribute concerts and continue to honor the legacy of the band and their music. Linkin Park's music continues to resonate with a diverse fan base, blending rock, rap, and electronic elements to create a unique sound that has left an indelible mark on the rock music landscape.
